RECIPE 143: Paneer Matar Makhana
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Paneer-Matar-Makhana---SK-Khazana.html

This vegetarian main course recipe is made with cottage cheese, green peas and lotus seeds cooked in a rich velvety gravy. You can enjoy this with hot paranthas, chapatis or rotis. This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: paneer (cottage cheese), Green peas | Cuisine: Indian,Fusion | Course: Main | Prep Time: 11-15 minutes | Cook Time: 26-30 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Mild | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 200 grams Paneer (cottage cheese) cut into small cubes
  - 1/4 cup Green peas boiled
  - 2 cups Lotus seeds
  - 4-5 Garlic cloves to taste Salt
  - 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds
  - 1 teaspoon Red chilli pepper
  - 1 tablespoon Coriander powder
  - 1 teaspoon Cumin powder
  - 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder
  - 1/4 teaspoon Garam masala powder
  - 1/4 teaspoon Dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi) powder
  - 2 tablespoons Fresh cream
  - 1 tablespoon Honey for garnish Fresh coriander sprigs to serve Paranthe to serve Onion wedges to serve Green chillies to serve Lemon wedges

METHOD:
  1. Heat 1 tsp ghee in a non-stick pan, add lotus seeds and saute on low heat for 5-6 minutes or till crisp.
  2. Transfer onto a plate and set aside.
  3. Heat 2 tsps ghee in the same pan, add onion and saute till golden brown.
  4. Add tomato, green chilli, ginger and garlic cloves and saute well.
  5. Add salt and mix well.
  6. Cover and cook for 5-6 minutes.
  7. Set aside to cool slightly.
  8. Transfer the mixture into a blender jar and add ¼ cup water and blend to a smooth paste.
  9. Heat remaining ghee in the same pan, add cumin seeds and sauté till their colour changes.
  10. Add the ground paste, mix well, cover and cook for 5-6 minutes.
  11. Add red chilli powder, coriander powder, cumin powder and turmeric powder, mix well and saute for a few seconds.
  12. Add ¾ cup water, mix, and cook for a minute.
  13. Add green peas, cottage cheese and the roasted lotus seeds and mix well.
  14. Sprinkle garam masala powder, dried fenugreek leaves powder and fresh cream and mix well.
  15. Add honey and mix and take the pan off the heat.
  16. Transfer into a serving bowl, garnish with coriander sprigs and serve hot with paranthe, onion wedges, green chillies and lemon wedges.

RECIPE 144: Paneer Parantha
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Paneer-Parantha.html

Paranthe can be stuffed with different stuffings. Paneer paranthe is one of them that is both healthy and delicious This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: Full cream milk, Fresh cream | Cuisine: Indian | Course: Breads | Prep Time: 2-2.30 hour | Cook Time: 26-30 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Mild | Level of Cooking: Moderate | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 litre full cream milk
  - 1 cup fresh cream
  - 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  - 1/2 cup grated processed cheese
  - 1 small onion , chopped
  - 2 green chillies , chopped
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
  - 1/2 teaspoons chaat masala
  - 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves Salt to taste Ghee for shallow frying

METHOD:
  1. To make paneer heat milk in a deep non-stick pan, add cream and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add vinegar and mix well.
  3. Keep a large sieve over a bowl and line it with a muslin cloth.
  4. When the milk curdles, pour it in the sieve and strain the excess whey.
  5. Gather the edges of the cloth together and put it in cold water.
  6. Remove from water, keep it again in the sieve and keep a heavy weight over it.
  7. Set aside for 2 hours to let all the water drain away.
  8. To make the stuffing, crumble the paneer into a bowl.
  9. Add cheese, onion, green chillies, red chilli powder, garam masala, chaat masala, carom seeds, fresh coriander leaves and salt and mix well.
  10. Divide the dough into equal portions.
  11. Shape into balls and press between your palms, make a cavity in the centre of each ball and add spoonful of paneer stuffing, bring the edges together and seal completely.
  12. Roll them out into five inch diameter paranthe.
  13. Heat a non-stick tawa, place one parantha at a time on it and cook for ½ minute.
  14. Flip it and apply ghee on the cooked side.
  15. Turn it over again and spread a little more ghee on the other side too.
  16. Cook, turning sides, till both sides are evenly cooked and golden brown.
  17. Wrap the paranthe in Freshwrapp and pack it in tiffin with yogurt packed in a small container with lid.

RECIPE 145: Pani Puri
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/PANI-PURI.html

Pani puri is a popular Indian street food snack, crispy puri shells filled with spicy, tangy flavored water (pani), along with a mixture of mashed potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and a tamarind chutney. This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: Puris, Potatoes | Cuisine: Indian | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 31-40 minutes | Cook Time: 0-5 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Tangy | Level of Cooking: Moderate | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1½ cups moong sprouts, blanched
  - 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  - 1 teaspoon chaat masala + for sprinkling
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder Black salt to taste Salt to taste ½ cup salted boondi
  - 2 cups ragda Fried papdis as required Sev for sprinkling Imli chutney
  - 30 minutes
  - 30 minutes ¼ cup melted jaggery
  - 1½ teaspoons red chilli powder Salt to taste Pudine ka pani
  - 1 small bunch fresh mint leaves (pudina) ½ small bunch fresh coriander leaves
  - 4-5 green chillies Salt to taste
  - 2 teaspoons roasted cumin powder
  - 2 teaspoons chaat masala Black salt to taste
  - 1 lemon Ice cubes as required

METHOD:
  1. To prepare imli chutney, drain the excess water from tamarind and dates.
  2. Strain the tamarind in a bowl and squeeze to get the pulp.
  3. Grind dates alongwith some water into a smooth and thick puree.
  4. Add the date puree, melted jaggery, cumin powder, chilli powder and salt to the tamarind pulp and mix.
  5. Add 1 cup water and mix well.
  6. To prepare pudine ka pani, separate the mint leaves from the sprigs.
  7. Blend together coriander sprigs, mint leaves and broken green chillies alongwith some water into a smooth mixture.
  8. Transfer in a bowl.
  9. Add salt, cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, pani puri masala, lemon juice and 4 cups water to the ground mint leaves mixture and mix well.
  10. Add few ice cubes and mix well.
  11. Mash the potatoes in another bowl.
  12. Add moong sprouts and mix.
  13. Add cumin powder, 1 teaspoon chaat masala, chilli powder, black salt and salt and mix well.
  14. Add salted boondi to pudine ka pani and mix well.
  15. Crack the top of each puri, put some radga, potato-moong mixture, pour some imli chutney and pudine ka pani and serve immediately.
  16. To prepare sukka puri, put some potato-moong mixture on fried puris, sprinkle some salt, chaat masala, black salt and sev on top and serve immediately.

RECIPE 161: Power Idli
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Power-Idli-Sanjeev-Kapoor-Kitchen-FoodFood.html

These idlis have neither rice nor rawa but only dals – healthy, tasty and an ideal breakfast snack. This recipe is from FoodFood TV channel

Main Ingredients: Split skinless black gram (dhuli urad dal), Split skinless green gram (dhuli moong dal) | Cuisine: South Indian | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 10-15 hour | Cook Time: 11-15 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Mild | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 cup split skinless black gram (dhuli urad dal), soaked
  - 1 cup split skinless green gram (dhuli moong dal), soaked
  - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds ½ teaspoon asafoetida
  - 1 teaspoon ginger-green chilli paste ½ teaspoon turmeric powder Salt to taste Coconut chutney to serve

METHOD:
  1. Drain and put split black gram and split green gram into a blender jar.
  2. Add sufficient water and blend to a fine paste.
  3. Transfer into a large bowl and whisk well.
  4. Cover the bowl and set aside in a warm place to ferment for 6-8 hours.
  5. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, ginger-green chilli paste, turmeric powder and salt and mix well.
  6. Heat sufficient water in a steamer, pour the batter into greased idli moulds and steam for 12-15 minutes.
  7. Take them off the heat.
  8. Dip spoon in water and scoop out the idlis from the mould and arrange them in a serving platter.
  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney.
