RECIPE 162: Protein Bowl
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/protein-bowl-7373685

Discover our ultimate 'Protein Bowl' recipe that’s both nutritious and flavorful. This bowl features a hearty mix of brown rice, grilled tofu, and colorful veggies. Ideal for a quick, healthy lunch, it’s packed with protein and taste.

Cuisine: Fusion | Course: Main | Cook Time: 25-30 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Mild Level of cooking Moderate | Diet: Vegetarian

INGREDIENTS:
  - 4 thick slices
  - 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  - 1 teaspoon onion powder
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli flakes
  - 1½ teaspoons lemon juice
  - 4 teaspoons olive oil Salt to taste
  - 2 tablespoons oil Herbed rice
  - 1 cup cooked brown rice
  - 1½ tablespoons finely chopped garlic ¾ teaspoon red chilli flakes
  - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint leaves
  - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  - 3 teaspoons chopped fresh mixed herbs Salt to taste Sautéed veggies
  - 4 teaspoons olive oil
  - 6-8 button mushrooms, thickly sliced
  - 12-15 medium broccoli florets, blanched and drained ½ cup mixed coloured capsicums triangles
  - 1 medium carrot, peeled, cut into triangles, boiled and drained
  - 1 medium onion, cut into quarters and layers separated Salt to taste ½ teaspoon chopped fresh mixed herbs Dressing
  - 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  - 2 tablespoons honey
  - 1 tablespoon mustard paste Salt to taste Crushed black peppercorns
  - 8-10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  - 20-25 assorted lettuce leaves
  - 4 tablespoons assorted micro greens + for garnish

METHOD:
  1. Place cottage cheese slices on a plate.
  2. Sprinkle garlic powder, onion powder, red chilli flakes, dried mixed herbs, lemon juice, olive oil and salt and apply the mixture well on all the slices and set aside to marinate for 10-15 minutes.
  3. To make the herbed rice, heat olive oil in a non-stick pan.
  4. Add garlic, mix and saute for a few seconds.
  5. Add red chilli flakes, mint leaves, coriander leaves, mixed herbs and salt and mix well.
  6. Add brown rice and mix well.
  7. Add ½ cup water, cover and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Take the pan off the heat.
  9. To make the sautéed veggies, heat olive oil in a non-stick pan, add garlic, mix and saute till golden brown.
  10. Add mushrooms, mix and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  11. Add broccoli, capsicums, carrots, and onion and mix well.
  12. Add salt and fresh herbs, mix well and saute for 1-2 minutes.
  13. Take the pan off the heat.
  14. To make the dressing, take extra virgin olive oil in a bowl, add honey, mustard paste, salt and crushed black peppercorns and whisk well.
  15. Add lemon juice and mix well.
  16. Add cherry tomatoes and English cucumber.
  17. Tear the assorted lettuce leaves and add to the bowl along with micro greens and gently toss till well combined.
  18. To grill the cottage cheese, heat oil in a non-stick grill pan.
  19. Place the cottage cheese slices on it and grill on high heat for 1-2 minutes.
  20. Flip and grill the other side for 1-2 minutes as well.
  21. To assemble, place a portion of the herbed rice in a serving bowl.
  22. Add a portion of the veggie mixture on one side, some of the salad on other side and place a cottage cheese slice on top.
  23. Garnish with micro greens and serve.

RECIPE 167: Punjabi Samosa
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/PUNJABI-SAMOSA.html

Samosa dough, shaped into a cone, stuffed with spicy potato pea mixture and deep fried. A popular snack from the Indian cuisine. This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: Refined flour (maida), Potatoes | Cuisine: Indian,Punjabi | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 31-40 minutes | Cook Time: 41-50 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Tangy | Level of Cooking: Difficult | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1½ cups refined flour
  - 4 medium potatoes, boiled and peeled ¼ teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain) Salt to taste
  - 4 tablespoons ghee
  - 3 tablespoons coriander seeds
  - 2 tablespoons cumin seeds
  - 1 tablespoon chopped ginger
  - 2-3 green chillies, chopped ½ cup boiled green peas ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
  - 2 tsps dried and crushed pomegranate seeds
  - 2 tsps dried mango powder (amchur)
  - 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves Oil for drizzling + for deep frying Green chutney to serve Date and tamarind chutney to serve Fried green chillies to serve

METHOD:
  1. Take refined flour in a parat.
  2. Add carom seeds, salt and mix well.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons ghee and mix well.
  4. Add 1 cup water and knead to stiff dough.Cover with a moist muslin cloth and set aside for 10 minutes.
  5. Dry roast coriander seeds on medium heat for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Transfer to a mortar pestle.
  7. Dry roast the cumin seeds in the same pan for 2 minutes on medium heat.
  8. Transfer in the mortar pestle and crush coarsely.
  9. For the stuffing, heat remaining ghee in a kadai.
  10. Add 1½ tablespoons of ground spice mix and sauté for a few seconds.
  11. Add ginger, green chillies and sauté for 30 seconds.
  12. Crush the potatoes and add to the kadai.
  13. Add the green peas and mix well.
  14. Add salt, garam masala powder, dried pomegranate seeds, dried mango powder and mix well.
  15. Add coriander leaves and mix well.
  16. Take the pan off the heat.
  17. Drizzle a little oil on a worktop and spread it evenly.
  18. Divide the dough into equal portions and shape each into a ball.
  19. For making samosa, roll the prepared balls in an oblong and cut in half to make a semi-circle.
  20. Take a semi-circle and apply water on the edges, shape into a cone.
  21. Stuff with a portion of the prepared stuffing and press the edges to seal.
  22. Heat sufficient oil in a kadai, deep fry the prepared samosas on medium heat till golden brown and crisp.
  23. Drain on an absorbent paper.
  24. Serve hot with green chutney, date and tamarind chutney and fried green chillies.

RECIPE 168: Pyaaz ki Kachori
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Pyaaz-ki-Kachori.html

Pyaaz ki Kachori is a traditional Rajasthani snack which satisfies your taste buds to the core. Its raw, authentic flavour is bound to make this kachori your favourite snack This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: Refined flour (maida) dough, Onions (pyaaz) | Cuisine: Rajasthani | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 31-40 minutes | Cook Time: 26-30 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Spicy | Level of Cooking: Moderate | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 2 medium onions (pyaaz), finely chopped
  - 1 tablespoon oil + for deep-frying
  - 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  - 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
  - 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  - 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder ½ teaspoon dried mango powder Salt to taste Crushed black peppercorns to taste
  - 1 tablespoon chopped fresh coriander leaves Date-tamarind chutney for serving

METHOD:
  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a non-stick pan.
  2. Add cumin seeds and sauté till it changes colour.
  3. Add ginger, garlic, green chilli, fennel seeds and coriander seeds and sauté for 1 minute.
  4. Add onion and sauté till light golden brown.
  5. Add chilli powder, dried mango powder and salt and sauté for 1 minute.

  13. Put the stuffed discs and deep-fry till golden brown and crisp.

  15. Serve hot with date-tamarind chutney.

  - 1 teaspoon garlic powder

  - 2 tablespoons oil + for deep fryingTo serve Fried chopped garlic Spring onion greens, diagonally sliced Fresh red chillies, diagonally sliced Soy-ginger dip to serve
