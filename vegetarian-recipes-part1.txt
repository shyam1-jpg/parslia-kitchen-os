RECIPE 1: Aam Papad Challi
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Aam-Papad-Challi-Turban-Tadka-FoodFood.html

A zesty fusion of tangy aam papad and crunchy nuts, delivering a burst of sweet and sour flavors in every delightful bite. This recipe is from FoodFood TV channel.

Main Ingredients: Regular aam papad, Kala aam papad | Cuisine: Indian | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 0-5 minutes | Cook Time: 11-15 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Sweet &amp; Sour | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 corn on cob Oil for drizzling Butter for brushing
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder Black salt to taste
  - 1 teaspoon chaat masala + for sprinkling
  - 2 tablespoons lemon juice

METHOD:
  1. Peel corn on cob and roast directly on high flame.
  2. Heat a non-stick grill pan.
  3. Cut roasted corn on cob into 3 portions, halve and place on the hot pan.
  4. Drizzle some oil and toss well.
  5. Brush the corn pieces with some butter, add red chilli powder, black salt, chaat masala and lemon juice.
  6. Top with regular and kala aam papad pieces and cook till soft.
  7. Transfer into a serving bowl, sprinkle chaat masala and serve immediately.

RECIPE 2: Almond Milk Smoothie
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Almond-Milk-Smoothie-Mummy-Ka-Magic-FoodFood.html

Enriched with almonds and garnished with sabza and blueberries, this smoothie is a power house of essential nutrients.

Main Ingredients: Almonds, Ripe bananas | Cuisine: Fusion | Course: Beverages | Prep Time: 6-7 hour | Cook Time: 0-5 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Sweet | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 cup almonds, soaked overnight and drained
  - 2 medium ripe banana
  - 1 cup fresh blueberries + for garnish
  - 1 teaspoon vanilla essence
  - 3 tablespoons honey Fresh mint sprigs for garnish
  - 2 tbsps basil seeds (sabza), soaked

METHOD:
  1. Put soaked almonds in a grinder jar, add 2 cups warm water and grind till mixture is smooth.
  2. Line a strainer kept on a bowl with muslin cloth, pour the ground almond mixture into it and strain the almond milk into the bowl.
  3. Grind the residue again with 1 cup warm water to get more almond milk and strain this into the same bowl.
  4. Peel the bananas, break into half and put in the grinder jar.
  5. Add blueberries, vanilla essence, honey, almond milk and grind to a smooth mixture.
  6. Skew blueberries and a mint sprig on each skewer.
  7. Put some basil seeds in each serving glass and pour the smoothie over them.
  8. Place one skewer on each glass and serve immediately.

RECIPE 3: Almond Phirni
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Almond-Phirni-Sanjeev-Kapoor-Kitchen-FoodFood.html

This recipe is specially for those who are intolerant to lactose, so almond milk is used instead of milk This recipe is from FoodFood TV channel

Main Ingredients: Almond milk, Rice | Cuisine: Indian | Course: Mithais | Prep Time: 1-1.30 hour | Cook Time: 26-30 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Sweet | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 3 cups Almond milk
  - 30 minutes and drained a large pi to garnish Saffron
  - 1/2 teaspoon Green cardamom powder
  - 3/4 cup Jaggery , chopped
  - 1/2 tablespoon Rose water to garnish Blanched pistachios , slivered

METHOD:
  1. Heat almond milk in a deep non-stick pan and let it come to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 8-10 minutes.
  3. Put rice in a blender jar, add ¼ cup water and blend to a coarse paste.
  4. Add this paste to almond milk and mix well.
  5. Add a large pinch of saffron and mix well and cook till the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens.
  6. Add green cardamom powder and mix well.
  7. Cook on low heat for 3-4 minutes, stirring constantly.
  8. Add jaggery and mix well.
  9. Cook till the jaggery is completely dissolved.
  10. Add rose water and mix well.
  11. Take the pan off the heat.
  12. Pour phirni into individual earthen bowls, sprinkle pistachio slivers on top and garnish with a few strands of saffron and serve chilled.

RECIPE 5: Aloo Muri
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/aloo-muri-8699704

Satisfy your snack cravings with 'Aloo Muri', a beloved street-side delight from Meghalaya. This irresistible recipe features crispy puffed rice, spiced mashed potatoes, a special homemade masala, and a drizzle of tangy mustard oil.

Cuisine: Meghalaya | Course: Snacks and Starters Prep time 25-30 minutes | Cook Time: 6-8 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Tangy Level of cooking Easy | Diet: Vegetarian

INGREDIENTS:
  - 3 medium potatoes (aloo), boiled and peeled
  - 4 cups puffed rice (muri)
  - 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
  - 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  - 1 medium onion, chopped
  - 2 green chillies, chopped
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder Black salt to taste Salt to taste ¼ cup tamarind water
  - 2-3 tablespoons mustard oil
  - 2-3 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves + to sprinkle ¼ cup aloo bhujiya + to sprinkle ½ lemon

METHOD:
  1. Heat a nonstick pan.
  2. Add coriander seeds, cumin seeds and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Transfer in a mortar and crush to fine powder with a mortar.
  4. Take a bowl, cut the potatoes into medium sized pieces and add to the bowl.
  5. Add onion, green chillies, puffed rice, 2-3 tbsps ground spice mix, tamarind water, mustard oil and mix well.
  6. Add coriander and aloo bhujiya and mix well.
  7. Squeeze lemon and mix.
  8. Transfer in serving bowls, sprinkle coriander, aloo bhujiya and serve immediately.

RECIPE 6: Anjeer and Boondi Pancakes
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Anjeer-and-Boondi-Pancakes--Turban-Tadka-FoodFood.html

A delectable fusion of traditional Indian flavors, featuring sweet figs and crunchy boondi, wrapped in a fluffy pancake for a delightful breakfast treat. This recipe is from FoodFood TV channel.

Main Ingredients: Dried figs, Sweet boondi | Cuisine: Fusion | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 41-50 minutes | Cook Time: 51-60 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Sweet | Level of Cooking: Moderate | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 8-10 dried figs, soaked and chopped ¼ cup sweet boondi
  - 1¾ cups milk
  - 15-20 walnuts Butter for shallow frying
  - 8 whole dried figs, soaked Rabri for garnish

METHOD:
  1. Take refined flour in a bowl, add baking soda and vanilla essence.
  2. Heat 1½ cups milk in a deep non-stick pan stirring continuously till the milk comes to a boil.
  3. Add jaggery, switch off heat and mix well till jaggery melts.
  4. Pour the milk mixture into the refined flour mixture.
  5. Add chopped figs, boondi and remaining milk.
  6. Add little water and mix well to make a medium thick and smooth batter.
  7. Chop and add walnuts and mix well.
  8. Set aside to rest for 10 minutes.
  9. Heat butter in a non-stick tawa, pour a ladle of batter and spread into a medium sized pancake.
  10. Place a whole fig in centre of and cook on medium heat for 2-3 minutes, turnover and cook on other side till golden brown on both sides.
  11. Transfer onto a serving plate and garnish with rabri.
  12. Serve immediately.

INGREDIENTS:
  - 2-3 hours and drained

  - 4-5 Walnuts
  - 2 tablespoons Cashewnuts , chopped
  - 2 tablespoons Pistachios , chopped
  - 2 tablespoons Almonds , chopped
  - 1/2 cup Gum resin (gond) , fried

METHOD:
  1. Put the figs in a grinder jar, add a little water and grind to fine paste.
  2. Heat ghee in a deep non-stick pan and add fig paste and sauté on medium heat for 3-4 minutes.

  9. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  10. Grease your palms with ghee and shape the mixture into oblong or any other desired shape.
  11. Take strips of parchment paper and place a toffee on each paper and roll them.
  12. Take coloured toffee sheets and roll the toffees again.
  13. Store the toffees in a glass jar or serve.

INGREDIENTS:

  - 110 grams almonds, soaked overnight and peeled ¼ cup butter, cut into cubes ½ teaspoon cinnamon powder ½ teaspoon vanilla essence ½ tablespoon lemon juice
  - 2 eggs
  - 1 tablespoon flax seeds (alsi)
  - 1 tablespoon chocolate chips

METHOD:
  1. Preheat oven to 110º C.

  9. Pour the mixture into a silicon loaf mould, level the surface and sprinkle chocolate chips on top.
  10. Place the mould in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes.
  11. Check the bread by inserting a toothpick in the centre and if it comes out clean the bread is done.
  12. Remove the mould from the oven and let it cool.
  13. Slice the bread.
  14. Wrap the slices in Freshwrapp and pack it in the tiffin box.

RECIPE 23: Bhakri with Thecha
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Bhakri-with-Thecha-Sanjeev-Kapoor-Kitchen-FoodFood.html

Typical rural meal of Maharashtra – it is so very tasty that you will want to eat it again and again This recipe is from FoodFood TV channel

Main Ingredients: Bhakri, Finger millet (nachni/ragi) flour | Cuisine: Maharashtrian | Course: Breads | Prep Time: 21-25 minutes | Cook Time: 31-40 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Spicy | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 cup + for dusting Finger millet (nachni/ragi) flour to taste Salt Thecha
  - 3 tablespoons Oil
  - 10-
  - 10-12 Light green chilliess to taste Sea salt
  - 1/4 cup Roasted peanuts

METHOD:
  1. To make bhakri, heat sufficient water in an earthen pot, and let it come to a boil.
  2. Take ragi flour in a large bowl, add salt and mix well.
  3. Pour the hot water little at a time, mix well till it forms soft dough.
  4. Divide the dough into equal portions and shape each into a ball.
  5. Dust each ball with ragi flour, flatten it and roll into a thin disc.
  6. Heat an earthen tawa, place the discs on it, one at a time, and cook, turning sides, till small brown specks appear on both sides.
  7. Take it off the heat and keep under a muslin cloth.
  8. To make thecha, heat oil in another earthen pot, add garlic, dark green chillies, light green chillies and sea salt and sauté well.
  9. Cover and cook for 12-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  10. Remove the lid and add roasted peanuts, adjust salt and sauté well.
  11. Cook for 2-3 minutes and take it off the heat.
  12. Cool the mixture, transfer into a mortar pestle and pound it coarsely.
  13. Arrange the bhakris on a serving platter and transfer thecha into a bowl and serve the two together.
