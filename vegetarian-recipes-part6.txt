RECIPE 116: Masala Aloo Kulcha + Tamatar ki Chutney
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Masala-Aloo-Kulcha-+-Tamatar-ki-Chutney-Turban-Tadka-FoodFood.html

Kulchas stuffed with a spicy potato mixture and cooked and served with a pungent tomato chutney – absolutely delicious This recipe is from FoodFood TV channel

Main Ingredients: Whole wheat flour (atta), Potatoes, Tomatoes | Cuisine: Indian | Course: Snacks and Starters | Prep Time: 31-40 minutes | Cook Time: 16-20 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Spicy | Level of Cooking: Moderate | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta) ½ cup refined flour (maida) + for dusting
  - 2½ tablespoons Oil + as required Filling
  - 2 medium potatoes, boiled and peeled
  - 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera)
  - 1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
  - 2-3 green chillies, finely chopped
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
  - 1 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur)
  - 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  - 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh coriander leaves
  - 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh mint leaves
  - 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  - 1 tablespoon oilTamatar ki chutney
  - 2 medium tomatoes (tamatar), finely chopped
  - 3 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  - 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
  - 1 teaspoon dried mango powder (amchur)
  - 1 teaspoon chaat masala Juice of ½ lemon
  - 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh coriander leaves
  - 2 teaspoons sugar

METHOD:
  1. Mix together wheat flour, refined flour, carom seeds, salt and 2 tablespoons oil in a parat.
  2. Add water as required and knead a soft dough.
  3. Add remaining oil, knead and set aside.
  4. To make the filling, dry roast coriander seeds and cumin seeds till fragrant.
  5. Crush in a mortar with pestle to a coarse powder and put in a bowl.
  6. Add ginger, green chillies, chilli powder, dried mango powder, onion, coriander leaves, mint leaves, potatoes and garam masala powder.
  7. Mash and mix well.
  8. Add oil and mix again.
  9. To make the tamatar ki chutney, mix together tomatoes, tomato ketchup, chilli powder, dried mango powder, chaat masala, lemon juice, coriander leaves and sugar in a bowl.
  10. Place an aluminium kadai upside down on heat.
  11. Divide the dough into equal portions and brush with some oil.
  12. Stuff each with a portion of the filling and shape into balls.
  13. Dust the worktop with some refined and sprinkle some coriander leaves on it.
  14. Place the stuffed balls on it and flatten them into discs.
  15. Apply some oil on the inverted kadai, stick the kulchas on it and roast till fully done, basting with oil.
  16. Serve hot with tamatar ki chutney.

RECIPE 117: Masala Rawa Puri
========================================================================
URL: https://www.sanjeevkapoor.com/Recipe/Masala-Rawa-Puri.html

These super crisp puris are made with a dough of rawa and some spice powders This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: Fine semolina (barik rawa), Ginger-green chilli paste | Cuisine: Maharashtrian | Course: Breads | Prep Time: 26-30 minutes | Cook Time: 11-15 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Spicy | Level of Cooking: Easy | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 cup Fine semolina (barik rawa)
  - 2 teaspoons Ginger-green chilli paste
  - 1 teaspoon Red chilli powder
  - 1 teaspoon Coriander powder
  - 1/2 teaspoon Cumin powder
  - 1/4 teaspoon Turmeric powder to taste Salt for greasi to deep fry Oil to serve Chai

METHOD:
  1. Put the semolina in a mixer jar and grind to a fine powder.
  2. Transfer into a large bowl.
  3. Add ginger-green chilli paste, red chilli powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder and salt and mix well.
  4. Add ½ cup water and knead into soft dough.
  5. Cover with a damp muslin cloth and set aside for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Grease the worktop with some oil.
  7. To make puris, divide the dough into small equal portions and shape them into balls.
  8. Roll out each ball, on the greased worktop, into a small thin disc.
  9. Heat sufficient oil in a kadai.
  10. Gently slide the discs, one at a time, into the hot oil and deep fry till they puff up and turn crisp.
  11. Drain on absorbent paper.
  12. Arrange them on a serving platter and serve hot with chai.

  - 1 tablespoon Dried figs chopped
  - 1 tablespoon Almonds chopped
  - 1 tablespoon Dates chopped

  - 1 tablespoon oil
  - 1 tbsp +
  - 1 teaspoon ghee
  - 1/4 cup raisins

  - 1 tablespoon finely chopped ginger
  - 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic

  - 1 tablespoon garam masala powder
  - 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  - 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  - 3 tablespoons roughly crushed cashewnuts
  - 1 cup milk
  - 2-3 green chillies , finely chopped

METHOD:

  12. Switch on heat, add remaining ghee and green chillies to the same pan.
  13. Add remaining milk to the ground mixture and add the mixture to the pan.
  14. Rinse the blender jar with ½ cup water and add to the remaining gravy.
  15. Add ½ cup water to the gravy and mix well.
  16. Add the fried potatoes, half the fried cashewnuts, 1 tbsp fried raisins and mawa, mix and cook on medium heat for 4-5 minutes.
  17. Transfer into a serving plate, garnish with mawa, remaining fried cashewnuts and remaining fried raisins.
  18. Serve hot.

========================================================================

Arachuvitta means freshly ground and this traditional sambhar from Tamil nadu is made with freshly ground masala This is a Sanjeev Kapoor exclusive recipe.

Main Ingredients: Onion, Green capsicum | Cuisine: Tamil Nadu | Course: Dals and Kadhis | Prep Time: 16-20 minutes | Cook Time: 31-40 minutes | Serve: 4 | Taste: Spicy | Level of Cooking: Moderate | Diet: Veg

INGREDIENTS:
  - 1 medium Onion , thickly sliced
  - 1 medium Green capsicum , thickly sliced
  - 1/2 cup Split pigeon peas (tuvar dal) , pressure cooked with ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  - 10-12 Curry leaves
  - 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida
  - 1 lemon si Tamarind , soaked in
  - 20 minutes
  - 1 tablespoon Jaggery , chopped
  - 2 tablespoons Fresh coriander leaves , chopped Masala paste
  - 1/4 cup Coriander seeds
  - 1 tablespoon Cumin seeds
  - 2 tablespoons Split pigeon peas (tuvar dal)
  - 1 tablespoon Split Bengal gram (chana dal)
  - 1/2 cup Fresh coconut , scraped
  - 5-6 Dried red chillies (pandi) Tempering
  - 1 tablespoon Ghee
  - 5-6 Curry leaves

METHOD:
  1. To make the masala paste, dry roast coriander seeds and cumin seeds till fragrant in a non-stick pan.
  2. Transfer onto a plate and set aside to cool.
  3. Similarly, dry roast pigeon peas and Bengal gram in the same pan till they turn golden brown.
  4. Transfer onto the same plate and let them cool.
  5. Dry roast coconut in the same pan till it turns golden brown.
  6. Transfer onto the same plate and let it cool.
  7. Heat oil in the same pan, add the dried red chillies and saute for a minute.
  8. Transfer onto the same plate and let them cool completely.

  10. To make the sambhar, heat oil in a non-stick deep pan.
  11. Add fenugreek seeds and 5-6 curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds.
  12. Add the onion and sauté till translucent.
  13. Add the capsicum and sauté till the onions turn golden brown.
  14. Add turmeric powder, sambhar masala and salt and sauté for a minute.
  15. Squeeze the soaked tamarind to extract the pulp and add along with the water and mix well.
  16. Let the mixture come to a boil.
  17. Continue to cook for 10-12 minutes.
  18. Add the ground paste and mix well.
  19. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  20. Add the cooked pigeon peas and mix.
  21. Add the remaining curry leaves and mix well.
  22. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  23. Add jaggery and mix, cook till it melts.
  24. Add coriander leaves and mix well.
  25. To make the tempering, heat ghee in a small pan.
  26. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  27. Add asafoetida and curry leaves and mix.
  28. Pour this into the sambhar and mix well.
  29. Serve hot with steamed rice.
